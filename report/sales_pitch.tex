\textbf{The pitch in one line.} We can match the European value-weighted
benchmark within 38\,bp of annualised geometric return while halving its
carbon footprint --- or run a full Paris-aligned Net-Zero glide path for
even less (-32\,bp).
\end{tcolorbox}

\textbf{What we built.}
A long-only annually rebalanced equity strategy on the
European Datastream universe (633 firms, 469--600 investable per year).
Five portfolios run side by side from January 2014 to December 2025:
the textbook minimum-variance and value-weighted benchmarks, plus three
carbon-constrained variants ($P^{mv}(0.5)$, $P^{vw}(0.5)$ and the
Paris-aligned $P^{vw}(NZ)$, decarbonising 10\% per year from the 2013
baseline).

\textbf{Three structural reasons it works.}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.4em,nosep]
  \item \emph{Right-skewed carbon distribution.} Ten European firms
        (Holcim, Rio Tinto, RWE, Air Liquide, Fortum, Enel, Heidelberg
        Materials, Endesa, EDF, Drax) deliver 44\% of the benchmark's
        weighted-average carbon intensity in 2013. Selectively
        underweighting them is enough to halve the carbon footprint
        with negligible tracking error.
  \item \emph{Self-decarbonisation tailwind.} The European value-weighted
        carbon footprint fell from 214 to 90 \mbox{tCO$_2$/M\$} between
        2013 and 2024 without any portfolio intervention (EU ETS pricing,
        coal-to-gas substitution, technology bull market). A glide-path
        strategy gets the first five years almost for free.
  \item \emph{Carbon premium is small and negative in our sample.}
        Low-carbon European stocks marginally outperformed during
        2014--2025, so divesting high-carbon names does not forfeit a
        risk premium. We do \emph{not} extrapolate this conclusion to
        other geographies or sample windows.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Who this is for.}
Institutional investors with European equity mandates and a Paris-aligned
or Article-9 commitment (pension funds, insurance general accounts,
sustainability-oriented asset managers). The strategy mirrors the spirit
of the EU Climate Transition Benchmark while offering a 50\% one-shot
variant for clients with stricter immediate carbon targets.

\textbf{What we are \emph{not} selling.}
This is a beta product, not alpha. Tracking error vs.\ $P^{vw}$ is
intentionally small. Outperformance over the value-weighted benchmark
should not be the headline KPI --- carbon savings per unit of tracking
error should.

\textbf{Next steps.}
(1) Plug in a Ledoit--Wolf shrinkage covariance for production stability.
(2) Add Scope\,3 once CSRD reporting becomes universal in 2025--2026.
(3) Test the strategy on the US (Russell 1000) and Emerging Markets
universes, where the carbon premium is empirically larger and the cost
of decarbonisation likely higher.
